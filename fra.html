Field Reliability Analysis (FRA)

Forensic root cause execution to eliminate "No Fault Found" loops and permanently close regulatory CAPAs with objective physics.

Scope of Work & Methodology

The objective of an FRA is to transition from subjective field complaints to objective, physics-based evidence. We isolate the physical failure mode, execute a 5-Whys logic progression, and provide the engineering data required to formally close an ISO 13485 Clause 8.5 CAPA.

Phase 1 & 2: Quarantine & Baseline

Contextual alignment of CRM/ERP failure logs and extraction of raw telemetry. Capture of external physical states, thermal imprints, and execution of IEC 62353 electrical baselines.

Phase 3: Invasive Teardown

Comparative disassembly against a Known Good Unit (KGU) to capture break-torque metrics, seal integrity, and isolation barrier deviations.

Phase 4: Root Cause Determination

Application of the 5-Whys and Ishikawa methodologies to definitively classify the fault as Design (DfM/DfS), Manufacturing, Use-Error, or Service execution.

Phase 5 & 6: Reporting & VoE

Re-commissioning of the failed asset to a safe state, followed by the translation of metrology data into a regulatory-grade report, including the strict Verification of Effectiveness (VoE) metric.

Liability Clause (Spoliation of Evidence)

The client must provide a Decontamination Certificate strictly limiting cleaning to a surface-level wipe. Units subjected to deep internal cleaning, fluid flushing, or depot-level "tinkering" prior to handover will be rejected, as this destroys forensic evidence (e.g., fluid ingress tidemarks, thermal deformation).
