Service Network Architecture (SNA)

Scale without headcount. We build the objective, ISO 13485 compliant turnkey architecture required to safely hand over hardware support to third-party depots.

Scope of Work & Methodology

Schema Technical operates as your outsourced engineering agent to establish a legally defensible third-party repair network. This engagement strictly bounds the execution of vendor qualification, curriculum engineering, and objective technician certification.

Phase 0: Technical Discovery

Rapid teardown and triage of the physical device to validate mechanical complexity if a formal Service Design Review (SDR) has not been previously executed.

Phase 1: Vendor Qualification (ISO 13485 Cl 7.4)

Remote screening of prospective depot capabilities via RFI, followed by the execution of a formal physical site audit to validate facility ESD controls, calibration limits, IT security, and inventory segregation.

Phase 2: Curriculum Engineering

Translation of the client's released Service Manual into a structured Master Training Matrix, engineering binary, objective Pass/Fail grading rubrics for all practical assessments.

Phase 3: Implementation & Certification

Deployment of a Lead Systems Engineer for up to 4 days on-site at the target depot. Delivery of the "Train the Trainer" cohort utilizing the 4-Stage ISD model, culminating in signed Training Records (ST-REC-TRN-001).

Liability Clause (Controlled Source Data)

The Client must provide formally released, QARA-approved Service Manuals prior to Phase 2. Schema Technical will not build training matrices based on verbal R&D intent or draft documentation. The Client QARA Director must formally sign and approve the Master Training Matrix before our trainers step onto the depot floor.
