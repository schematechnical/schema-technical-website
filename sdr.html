Service Design Review (SDR)

Forensic pre-freeze hardware auditing. We dismantle your prototype to identify margin-destroying field failures before you commit to tooling.

Scope of Work & Deliverables

The Service Design Review (SDR) is a highly forensic, physical engineering audit executed prior to your final design freeze. The objective is to intercept unmitigated physical risks, routing failures, and access bottlenecks that will cause your Mean Time To Repair (MTTR) to spike in the field.

Phase 1: Forensic Teardown

Schema Technical will execute a complete mechanical disassembly of one (1) physical, production-equivalent device. We map the accessibility and physical friction associated with reaching your top 5 highest-risk Field Replaceable Units (FRUs).

Phase 2: Formal Audit Report (REP-SDR-001)

We deliver a strict, regulatory-grade engineering dossier detailing:

  • Downtime Metrics: Calculating the exact MTTR for specific components based on fastener count and loom obstruction.
  • Red Flag Interventions: Immediate, actionable CAD revisions required prior to tooling (e.g., trapped cables, blinded captive nuts, missing IP seals).
  • Special Tooling Demands: Identifying areas where proprietary jigs are required due to poor sub-assembly alignment.

Phase 3: Executive Debrief

A comprehensive video conference with your R&D and Quality leadership to present the physical evidence and agree on the required Engineering Change Orders (ECOs) before mass production begins.

Liability Clause (Blind Auditing)

Schema Technical does not conduct SDRs purely from CAD files. We require physical access to a prototype. Assessing cable flex, real-world tolerance stacking, and human ergonomics requires a physical bench audit to guarantee accuracy.
