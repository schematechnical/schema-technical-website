The Information for Safety Mandate

ISO 14971
Risk Management

*When a physical hazard cannot be eliminated by design, the final layer of protection is "Information for Safety."

Manuals are not guidelines. They are Risk Controls.

In the MedTech sector, a Service Manual is a legally binding regulatory document. If a manual is vague, the resulting hazard is a direct failure of the OEM's QMS.

Drafting these documents cannot be delegated to junior staff using basic word processors. They must be engineered.

The 5-Phase Authoring Architecture

1: Intake

Securing RMF, 3D CAD geometry, and production-equivalent hardware.

2: Anatomy

Reconciling hardware against CAD and engineering safe-state sequences.

3: Syntax

Drafting using verb-first imperatives and ISO 3864 hazard signal words.

4: Validation

Executing drafts physically on the bench to prove tooling clearances.

5: Handover

Final QARA review and ingestion into the Digital Service Portal.

Eliminating "Desk-Drafted" Manuals

The Flawed Approach

Traditional technical writers often author manuals entirely from 3D CAD models while sitting at a desk, ignoring tooling clearances and ergonomics.

The Physical Validation Mandate

Schema Technical executes every drafted procedure on physical hardware on our engineering bench:

  • Validates specified torque tools physically fit.
  • Identifies unmitigated sharp edges.
  • Ensures sequence flow matches physical reality.

Linguistic Hazard Control

Mitigating Foreseeable Use Error

We apply strict grammatical architecture to eliminate cognitive ambiguity during high-stress field interventions.

We utilise Verb-First Imperative Syntax and explicitly apply ISO 3864 Signal Words (DANGER, WARNING, CAUTION) immediately prior to the hazard.
