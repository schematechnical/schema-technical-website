The Information for Safety Mandate

ISO
14971
RISK MANAGEMENT
"When a physical hazard cannot be eliminated by R&D design, the absolute final layer of regulatory protection is 'Information for Safety.'"

Manuals are not guidelines. They are Risk Controls.

Under MDR and IVDR, a Service Manual is a legally binding regulatory document. If a field engineer pinches a high-voltage cable because the manual was vague, the resulting patient hazard is a direct failure of the OEM's Quality Management System.

Drafting these documents cannot be delegated to junior staff using basic word processors. They must be engineered.

The 5-Phase Authoring Architecture

1: Intake

Securing the Risk Management File, 3D CAD geometry, and production-equivalent hardware.

2: Anatomy

Reconciling physical hardware against CAD and engineering safe-state isolation sequences.

3: Syntax

Drafting using strict verb-first imperatives and injecting ISO 3864 hazard signal words.

4: Validation

Executing drafts physically on the bench to prove tooling clearances and generate vector art.

5: Handover

Final QARA review of technical limits and ingestion into the Digital Service Portal.

Eliminating "Desk-Drafted" Manuals

The Flawed Approach

Traditional technical writers often author manuals entirely from 3D CAD models while sitting at a desk. This results in instructions that are physically impossible to execute in the field because they ignore tooling clearances, real-world loom routing, and human ergonomics.

The Physical Validation Mandate

Schema Technical refuses to author blindly. We execute every drafted procedure on physical, production-equivalent hardware on our engineering bench:

  • Validates specified torque tools physically fit.
  • Identifies unmitigated sharp edges.
  • Ensures sequence flow matches physical reality.

Linguistic Hazard Control

Mitigating Foreseeable Use Error

We apply strict grammatical architecture to eliminate cognitive ambiguity during high-stress field interventions.

We utilise Verb-First Imperative Syntax (e.g., "Remove the screws", not "The screws should be removed"). We explicitly apply ISO 3864 Signal Words (DANGER, WARNING, CAUTION) immediately prior to the physical action that triggers the hazard, preventing technicians from skimming past vital safety limits.

CAD-Derived Vector Art

Generic photographs fade, photocopy poorly, and clutter the page. We generate high-contrast vector line art derived directly from your .STEP files, highlighting specific interaction points with absolute visual clarity.

Solving ISO 13485 Clause 4.2.4

The "Local File"
Threat

If field engineers carry PDF manuals on their local hard drives, they will inevitably execute a repair using an obsolete, cached file. This is a critical Document Control failure.

Digital Service
Portals

We architect and configure secure SharePoint intranet portals. This forces engineers to access a single, globally updated source of truth, eradicating the risk of obsolete documentation.

View-Only
Enforcement

We configure complex permission matrices that allow field engineers to view manuals in the browser, but mathematically blocks them from downloading or printing the IP locally.
